personalised reading experience

the parts that matter. nothing else.

your research interests, encoded once. every paper you open, filtered through them. a quieter way to read.

enter → how it works

a master's project · KFUPM

manifesto

not every sentence deserves your time. researchers read hundreds of papers a year, and most of every paper is noise to you — even when it's signal to someone else. we built a reader that knows the difference, because it knows you.

how

three steps. about two minutes.

01

build your profile

pick research topics. add a few keywords. drop in one or two papers you already like. the system fuses them into a single signal of what you care about.

02

upload a paper

any text-based pdf. the system parses every sentence, scores each one against your profile, and keeps the rest aside.

03

read what matters

relevant sentences highlighted directly in the original document. real pdf, real annotations. download it. open it anywhere.

features

designed for serious reading.

multi-source profile

topics, keywords, seed papers, and free text fused via weighted-mean embeddings. coherent representation from messy signals.

semantic ranking

each sentence scored by cosine similarity against your profile vector. no keyword matching. no rules. just meaning.

annotated pdf output

the original paper, with your highlights. plus a rendered page viewer in-app. no rebuilding the reading experience from scratch.

profile reusable

build once, use on every paper that follows. profiles saved as portable json. take them anywhere.

off-profile fallback

when the paper doesn't match your interests, the system says so — and offers the best guesses anyway. honest, not silent.

local first

everything runs in your session. no accounts. no tracking. no cloud-side profile storage.

under the hood

technical detail, briefly.

embedding model

qwen3-embedding-8b (Custom Embedding Model from Qwen) on gpu · all-minilm-l6-v2 on cpu

pdf processing

pymupdf for extraction · annotation · page rendering

interface

modal · python · Vite

caching

model-aware embedding cache · per-document persistence
